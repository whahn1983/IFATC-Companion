Privacy at a glance

IFATC Companion is a deterministic, local-only air traffic control (ATC) companion for the flight simulator Infinite Flight. It is designed so that your data stays on your device and your local Wi-Fi network.

No account or login No backend server No AI / no LLM No analytics No advertising No tracking No in-app purchases No data sold

This Privacy Policy explains what information the App accesses, how it is used, the limited circumstances in which any information leaves your device, and the third-party services the App relies on. We have written it to meet the requirements of the Apple App Store, including Apple's App Store Review Guidelines and App Privacy ("nutrition label") requirements.

2. Summary of data practices

The App does not collect, store, or transmit personally identifiable information to us or to any analytics, advertising, or tracking service. There is no developer-operated backend, and we never receive your data. The App functions entirely on your device, except for two narrowly scoped network activities described below: (a) a connection to Infinite Flight on your local Wi-Fi network, and (b) requests to a public aviation-weather service for METAR/TAF/PIREP/SIGMET reports.

3. Information the App accesses and how it is handled

3.1 Flight and aircraft data from Infinite Flight (local network only)

When you use Live mode, the App connects to Infinite Flight running on another device on the same local Wi-Fi network using the Infinite Flight Connect API v2 over a direct TCP connection. Over this local connection the App reads your simulated flight data — for example aircraft position, altitude, heading, speed, vertical speed, ground state, flight phase, and your filed flight plan (departure, destination, route fixes, and procedures). It may optionally send Infinite Flight UNICOM actions that announce your own simulated intentions, if you enable UNICOM automation.

This is simulated flight data, not real-world location or travel data. It is exchanged only between your devices on your local network and is never sent to us or to any internet server. To discover Infinite Flight on the network the App uses Bonjour (_infiniteflight._tcp) and a local UDP discovery listener; these operate on your local network only.

3.2 Microphone and speech recognition (push-to-talk)

The App offers an optional push-to-talk feature so you can speak ATC readbacks and requests instead of tapping buttons. When you hold the push-to-talk control, the App captures microphone audio and uses Apple's Speech framework to transcribe it into text, which is then matched to an action by deterministic, on-device keyword rules.

  • The App requests on-device speech recognition whenever the device supports it, so that audio is processed locally and is not sent over the network.
  • On devices or locales where on-device recognition is not available, Apple's Speech framework may process the audio using Apple's servers, subject to Apple's Privacy Policy. The developer does not receive this audio or its transcription.
  • The App does not record, save, or transmit your microphone audio to the developer, and the recognized text is used transiently to drive the ATC conversation.

The microphone is used only while you actively hold push-to-talk. You can decline or revoke microphone and speech-recognition permissions at any time in iOS Settings; the App remains fully usable with button-based input.

3.3 Weather data requests (internet)

The Weather features fetch aviation weather (METAR, TAF, PIREP, and SIGMET reports) from the NOAA Aviation Weather Center public service at aviationweather.gov. These requests require no API key and no account. To request relevant reports, the App includes airport/station identifiers and, for route weather, route-related parameters derived from your simulated flight plan.

  • The requests contain aviation identifiers and simulated route information only — no name, email, account, device identifier, or personal contact information.
  • Like any internet request, NOAA may receive your device's IP address and standard request metadata as part of normal network operation. Your use of NOAA's service is subject to NOAA's own policies.
  • Weather results are cached on your device so the App can show recent data and reduce requests.

3.4 Maps

The Weather tab can display a route/weather map overlay using Apple's MapKit. Rendering maps may involve communication with Apple's mapping services to retrieve map tiles and related data, subject to Apple's Privacy Policy. The developer does not receive any data through this feature.

3.5 Text-to-speech

Spoken ATC and pilot calls are generated on-device using Apple's AVSpeechSynthesizer. This text-to-speech runs fully offline and sends nothing to the developer or any server.

3.6 Settings and on-device storage

Your preferences and in-progress session data are stored locally on your device (using iOS UserDefaults) and are not transmitted to us. This includes, for example:

  • Connection settings (the host/IP address and port you enter for Infinite Flight, auto-discovery, and the weather service URL).
  • Preferences such as selected voices, UNICOM automation mode, phraseology and unit settings, and keep-screen-awake.
  • User-created phraseology profiles (custom call templates and airline call names), which you can optionally export as JSON to share — sharing happens only if and when you choose to export and send a file.
  • A session snapshot and ATC conversation transcript used to resume an in-progress flight after a reconnect, stored locally.

This data remains on your device and is removed when you delete the App, and can be cleared from within the App (for example by resetting app data or clearing a flight).

5. Third-party services and integrations

The App relies on the following external or platform services. We do not share your personal information with them, and we receive no data back from them.

  • Infinite Flight Connect API v2 (Infinite Flight LLC) — local-network connection to the flight simulator. See Infinite Flight's privacy policy.
  • NOAA Aviation Weather Center (U.S. National Weather Service / aviationweather.gov) — public aviation weather data.
  • Apple frameworks — Speech (speech recognition), MapKit (maps), and AVSpeechSynthesizer (text-to-speech), provided by Apple Inc. See Apple's Privacy Policy.

The App contains no third-party advertising, analytics, attribution, crash-reporting, or social-media SDKs, and no third-party software development kits that collect data.

6. How we use information

Because the App does not transmit your data to us, we do not use your personal information for any purpose. On-device, the data described above is used solely to:

  • generate simulated, procedural ATC and pilot phraseology for your flight;
  • display your flight state, flight plan, and route weather;
  • recognize your spoken push-to-talk input to drive the conversation; and
  • remember your preferences and resume an in-progress session.

We do not sell, rent, or share personal information, and we do not use your data for advertising or for tracking you across apps or websites.

7. Data retention and deletion

All preferences, profiles, session snapshots, and transcripts are stored only on your device. You can:

  • clear in-app data using the App's reset / clear-flight controls;
  • revoke microphone, speech-recognition, or local-network permissions in iOS Settings; and
  • delete all locally stored data by deleting the App from your device.

We retain no copy of your data because we never receive it.

8. Children's privacy

The App is a flight-simulation companion intended for a general audience and is not directed to children under 13. The App does not knowingly collect personal information from children, and it contains no accounts, advertising, social features, or in-app purchases. If you believe a child has provided personal information through any feature, please contact us so we can address it.

9. Your privacy rights

Depending on where you live, you may have rights under privacy laws such as the EU/UK General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) or the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA/CPRA), including rights to access, correct, delete, or port personal data, and to opt out of the "sale" or "sharing" of personal data. Because IFATC Companion does not collect or transmit personal information to us and does not sell or share personal information, there is no developer-held personal data to access, correct, delete, or opt out of. You can exercise full control over the App's on-device data using the deletion steps in Section 7. If you have questions about your rights, contact us using the details below.

10. Security

The App minimizes privacy risk by design: it keeps data on your device, uses local-network connections for Infinite Flight, and relies on Apple's platform protections for on-device storage. Connections to Infinite Flight occur on your own local network; you control that network's security. No method of electronic storage or transmission is 100% secure, but because we do not collect or store your data on any server, there is no central repository of your information to be exposed.

11. International users

The App can be used worldwide. Weather requests are served by NOAA, a U.S. government service, and Apple services are operated by Apple. Using these services may involve processing in the United States or other countries under those providers' own policies. The developer does not transfer your personal data internationally because the developer does not receive it.
